Privacy Policy & Data Shield

Effective Date: January 1, 2026. This document governs all data storage, processing, and transfer practices globally.

1. General Provisions & Legal Foundations

This Global Privacy Policy (the "Policy") defines the institutional frameworks, guidelines, and compliance rules managed by Dexton Solvexx ("we," "our," "us," or "Dexton Solvexx") to secure and protect personal and professional information. We operate as an international entity providing high-performance recruitment process outsourcing, offshore staffing, executive search, and cross-border Employer of Record (EOR) services.

As an organization handling critical business data and professional profiles across multiple continents, Dexton Solvexx is committed to compliance with leading data protection laws. This includes the European Union's General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) as amended by the CPRA, the UK Data Protection Act 2018, and the Australian Privacy Principles (APP) under the Privacy Act 1988. We employ advanced technical measures and organizational controls to protect data security, integrity, and confidentiality across all operational systems.

2. Role Definitions: Data Controller vs. Data Processor

Under the GDPR and associated international privacy regimes, the legal status of Dexton Solvexx is determined by the specific context of the data processing:

Data Controller: When Dexton Solvexx collects and processes personal information from candidates, business prospects, web visitors, or employees to manage our primary sourcing platforms, run corporate marketing, or oversee payroll, we act as the Data Controller.

Data Processor: When we manage global staffing operations under an RPO agreement, processing candidate databases, timesheet logs, and platform details entirely on behalf of a client company, we act as the Data Processor. In these contexts, the client remains the Data Controller, and our processing is governed strictly by the terms of our customer service agreement.

3. Categories of Information Collected

Dexton Solvexx collects personal, professional, and technical data through several pathways, including website interactions, online forms, screening assessments, and phone interviews:

  • Identity Data: Full name, national identification details (where required for EOR compliance), passports copy, tax registration numbers, and visa status.
  • Contact Data: Corporate email addresses, telephone numbers, instant messaging identifiers, physical business addresses, and emergency contacts.
  • Professional & Experience Data: Resumes, portfolios, academic credentials, licensing information, salary history, career references, and results from technical coding evaluations.
  • Financial & Billing Data: Direct deposit details, bank accounts, international routing codes, corporate tax IDs, billing addresses, and invoice histories.
  • Technical & System Data: IP addresses, browser configurations, geographic location parameters, device tracking details, and platform access logs.

4. Processing Grounds & Legal Bases

We process personal and corporate data under clear legal bases, in compliance with GDPR Article 6 parameters:

  • Performance of a Contract: Processing required to execute employment contracts, manage client agreements, verify payroll records, and coordinate remote staffing services.
  • Consent: When candidates explicitly authorize us to process their profiles, submit resumes to target clients, or execute third-party background screenings.
  • Legitimate Interests: Sourcing top-tier professional profiles, optimizing our recruitment tools, maintaining website security, and delivering industry-focused marketing insights.
  • Legal Obligations: Meeting international tax compliance laws, labor rules, and cooperating with government audits or law enforcement investigations.

5. Cross-Border Data Transfers & Security Measures

As an international offshore staffing firm, Dexton Solvexx regularly transfers data between regional hubs in the US, UK, Europe, India, Singapore, and Latin America. We employ strict safeguards to secure these cross-border data flows:

We utilize **Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)** approved by the European Commission for transfers to countries without adequacy decisions. All transferred data is encrypted in transit using **TLS 1.3** and secured at rest using **AES-256** standards. Access is managed through strict role-based control systems and multi-factor authentication (MFA) protocols. Our local operational environments are managed under ISO 27001 parameters, protecting candidate and corporate information from unauthorized access.

6. Data Retention & Governance

Dexton Solvexx retains personal and corporate information only as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected. For candidates, this means keeping active profile details in our vetted databases for future opportunities, unless a deletion request is received. For clients, transaction records and contract documents are retained in compliance with local commercial, accounting, and tax regulations, typically ranging between 5 to 7 years after the termination of the service agreement.

7. Individual Rights: Access, Deletion, and Portability

In compliance with the GDPR and CCPA, individuals hold specific rights regarding their personal data, including:

  • Right of Access (DSAR): Request a detailed report of the personal information Dexton Solvexx holds about you.
  • Right to Rectification: Request corrections to inaccurate, incomplete, or out-of-date records.
  • Right to Erasure (The Right to be Forgotten): Request the permanent deletion of your personal data from our systems, subject to legal retention overrides.
  • Right to Portability: Request a machine-readable copy of your personal data for transfer to another platform.
  • Right to Object & Restrict: Withdraw processing consent or opt-out of marketing communications at any time.

To exercise your data protection rights, please contact our Data Protection Officer at **privacy@dextonsolvexx.com**. We respond to all verified requests within 30 days, without administrative charges.
